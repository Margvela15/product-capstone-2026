## Primary ICP: Fragmented Academic Communications causing Missed Deadlines

This ICP describes the person most likely to experience your committed problem most acutely. They are your primary interview target.

### 1. Segment

A 3rd-year undergraduate Computer Science student at Kutaisi International University (KIU) who is currently taking at least 3 project-based or heavily evaluated courses in the same semester. They are active in university group chats but consider themselves disorganized with files.

**Test:** Could you walk into a campus building and within 10 minutes identify someone matching this description? (Yes: Ask anyone in the CS study area or library what year they are).

---

### 2. Context

It is the middle of the semester. They are sitting in the library or their dorm with multiple IDEs and browser tabs open, trying to prioritize which assignment to start on for the week, while their phone continuously buzzes with Messenger/WhatsApp notifications from course groups asking about deadlines.

**Test:** Is this a specific enough context that you could design an interview question around it? (Yes: "Tell me about the last time you sat down to work on your assignments for the week.")

---

### 3. Primary Pain

The sudden panic of realizing an assignment is due tonight at 23:59, or reading the syllabus after submitting and realizing they missed a specific formatting requirement that was posted exclusively as a Teams announcement instead of on the actual Moodle submission page. It feels like "fighting the system" instead of learning.

**Test:** Would someone matching this ICP read this description and say "that is exactly what it feels like"? (Yes).

---

### 4. Current Workaround

They rely heavily on the "Course Representative" or more organized friends in group chats to compile a list of requirements. They take screenshots of Teams announcements and leave them in their phone gallery, and manually click through every single active course module on Moodle every 1-2 days.

---

### 5. Job to Be Done

"When I am juggling multiple ongoing university deliverables, I want to see a unified, prioritized list of all scattered actionable requirements, so I can start my actual coursework without the anxiety of missing a hidden rule or deadline."

---

### 6. Success Signal

They would stop repeatedly asking peers "did we have something due today?" in group chats. They would stop opening platforms (Teams, Moodle) solely to check for updates out of fear. They would feel in control of their academic schedule.

---

### 7. Where to Find Them

- The KIU campus library during peak afternoon hours
- KIU dorm study rooms in the evening
- 3rd-year CS course group chats on Messenger/WhatsApp where we can drop a quick interview request survey.

---

### 8. Why You, Why Now

We are also 3rd-year KIU CS students facing the exact same problem right now. We have immediate rapport and empathy with them, meaning they will give us honest feedback without feeling judged about being disorganized.

## Secondary ICP: Lost Decisions and Tasks in Group Projects

This ICP describes the person most likely to experience your second-ranked problem. You are keeping this alive as a backup in case interview evidence undermines your primary commitment.

### 1. Segment

A KIU student (any major, though frequently CS or Business) who often acts as the unofficial "Team Lead" or coordinator in 3-to-5 person group projects.

---

### 2. Context

They are trying to assemble the final presentation or codebase the night before submission, only to realize a team member failed to complete their part because the assignment of that specific role got buried 200 messages up in a Messenger group chat.

---

### 3. Primary Pain

Deep frustration and resentment at having to do everything themselves at the last minute because someone else dropped the ball due to poor task visibility.

---

### 4. Current Workaround

Using the "Reply" feature in Messenger constantly to refer to old tasks. Occasionally trying to force the group to use Trello or a shared Google Doc checklist, though 70% of the time teammates ignore it and report back in chat anyway.

---

### 5. Job to Be Done

"When I am collaborating on a graded group assignment, I want an integrated way to persistently attach task ownership to conversations, so I can hold my teammates accountable without feeling like a micromanager."

---

### 6. Success Signal

They stop needing to manually consolidate who is doing what every 2 days. The team naturally executes their parts without the "coordinator" constantly pinging them.

---

### 7. Where to Find Them

KIU project collaboration spaces (e.g. computer labs, library discussion rooms) when a major group project milestone is approaching for mandatory courses.

---

### 8. Why You, Why Now

As peers who work in these exact teams, we've experienced both sides—being the frustrated team lead and the confused teammate. We are accessible and understand the specific KIU course dynamic.
