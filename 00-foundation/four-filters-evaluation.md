## Scoring Reference

For each filter, score 1 to 3:
- **3** = Clear evidence this passes
- **2** = Possible pass, but uncertain — needs more information
- **1** = Fails this filter

| Filter | What it measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| Real and Recurring | Happens often enough to justify building a solution |
| Painful Enough to Act | People are already trying to solve it (workarounds exist) |
| Underserved Today | Existing solutions are genuinely inadequate |
| Accessible to You | You can interview users this week and build for this space |

## Evaluations (Deep-dive on Top 4 Candidates)

### Problem 3: Missed deadlines due to fragmented platforms 

**Real and Recurring**
- Score: 3/3
- Justification: KIU students take multiple project-based courses concurrently every semester. Professors consistently use different combinations of Moodle, Teams, and email.

**Painful Enough to Act**
- Score: 3/3
- Known workaround: Students constantly message each other in WhatsApp/Messenger groups asking "is anything due?" or "did the professor post the requirement?". They perform daily manual sweeps of Moodle and Teams.
- Justification: The fear of losing grades (academic penalty) actively drives students to spend significant time cross-checking information.

**Underserved Today**
- Score: 3/3
- Existing solutions: Moodle Calendar, personal planners, Teams notifications. They are inadequate because they aren't unified, and informal announcements dropped in chats are never captured globally.
- Justification: Not a single official KIU platform aggregates everything flawlessly without active manual effort from the student.

**Accessible to You**
- Score: 3/3
- Who could you interview this week: Classmates in KIU taking 3+ CS courses, students in the library or dorms.
- Justification: Team members are all KIU students and have direct access to hundreds of peers experiencing the exact same issue.

**Total: 12/12**
**Recommendation:** Strong candidate

### Problem 6: Dorm responsibility sharing conflicts

**Real and Recurring**
- Score: 3/3
- Justification: Every student living in shared dorms has to deal with chores and shared expenses daily or weekly.

**Painful Enough to Act**
- Score: 2/3
- Known workaround: Verbal agreements, sticky notes on fridges, using Splitwise for expenses.
- Justification: Painful, but often people just ignore the mess or one person gives up and cleans it. The financial aspect acts faster than the chore aspect.

**Underserved Today**
- Score: 2/3
- Existing solutions: Splitwise works perfectly for money, but not for chore tracking or grocery inventory.
- Justification: There are chore apps (Sweepy, Nipto), but students rarely adopt them long-term because they require too much manual input. 

**Accessible to You**
- Score: 3/3
- Who could you interview this week: Nikoloz's roommates, friends living in KIU dormitories.
- Justification: Direct proximity to KIU dorm residents.

**Total: 10/12**
**Recommendation:** Possible candidate

---

### Problem 9: Lost decisions/tasks in group projects

**Real and Recurring**
- Score: 3/3
- Justification: Every KIU CS student has to work in groups for final projects, ensuring continuous exposure to this problem.

**Painful Enough to Act**
- Score: 2/3
- Known workaround: Scrolling up in Messenger/WhatsApp, making Notion/Trello boards.
- Justification: The pain happens mid-project, though at the start people feel fine. The workaround (scrolling) is annoying but free. 

**Underserved Today**
- Score: 2/3
- Existing solutions: Trello, Asana, Notion. Inadequate because they are too "heavy" for a quick 3-person university project; students default back to Messenger.
- Justification: The market has many tools, the underlying problem is student discipline, making it risky to build *another* project management tool.

**Accessible to You**
- Score: 3/3
- Who could you interview this week: Students working on capstone projects, teammates.
- Justification: The team itself is doing a project and can easily survey the class.

**Total: 10/12**
**Recommendation:** Possible candidate / Backup

---

### Problem 12: Missed classes due to uncoordinated schedule changes

**Real and Recurring**
- Score: 2/3
- Justification: Schedule changes happen, but normally concentrated at the beginning of the semester or during exceptional circumstances.

**Painful Enough to Act**
- Score: 2/3
- Known workaround: Relying on the group "class representative" to announce changes in WhatsApp. 
- Justification: Painful when it happens (wasted commute, missed content), but relatively infrequent compared to daily homework management.

**Underserved Today**
- Score: 2/3
- Existing solutions: Official university timetables, email blasts. Inadequate when lecturers make last-minute changes.
- Justification: Hard to solve technically without university admin integration.

**Accessible to You**
- Score: 3/3
- Who could you interview this week: Class representatives, commuting students.
- Justification: Easily found before or after lectures.

**Total: 9/12**
**Recommendation:** Drop / Pivot into Problem 3

## Shortlist

Based on the scores above, our top 2-3 candidates are:

1. **Missed deadlines due to fragmented platforms (Problem 3)** — Score: 12/12 — It perfectly marries high recurring pain (grade loss), daily student stress, and a lack of unified tooling across KIU's Moodle/Teams/Chat ecosystem.
2. **Lost decisions/tasks in group projects (Problem 9)** — Score: 10/12 — A massive recurring headache for our exact demographic, making testing and iterating highly accessible.
3. **Dorm responsibility sharing conflicts (Problem 6)** — Score: 10/12 — Represents a totally different angle (lifestyle vs academic) that still scores highly on accessibility and recurring friction.

## Team Discussion Notes

Record any important points of disagreement or debate that came up during scoring. What did the team argue about? How was it resolved?

- The team debated whether "Lost decisions in group projects" (9) was underserved. Some argued Trello already exists. We resolved that Trello is abandoned by students because it causes friction, leaving the problem functionally underserved *for students*.
- We also noticed that Archil's and Giorgi K's problems are very similar (fragmentation of information). We decided to combine the essence of "information scattered across platforms" into our main focus, pivoting heavily on the academic deadline pain over the classroom location pain, as it carries more severe consequences (grades).

## Decision

Our committed problem for Lab 2 is:

**Fragmented Academic Communications causing Missed Deadlines** (Derived from Problem 3 & Problem 1)

This problem was chosen because:
It received a perfect 12/12. It carries the highest consequence (grade reduction), happens continuously, and the current workarounds (manual sweeping of 3+ platforms) are incredibly tedious and error-prone. We have immense accessibility to this user group.

The runner-up problem we are keeping alive as our secondary ICP candidate is:

**Lost decisions/tasks in group projects (Problem 9)**
