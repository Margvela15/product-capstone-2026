## The Problem Statement

> Third-year Computer Science students at KIU taking multiple project-based courses experience heightened academic stress and avoidable grade reductions when managing overlapping coursework requirements across unintegrated platforms (Moodle, Teams, WhatsApp), causing them to submit assignments late or miss required formatting guidelines. Currently, they cope by continuously manually checking all three platforms and relying on peers in chat groups to remind them, which is inadequate because the sheer volume of messages and inconsistency of faculty platform usage mean critical updates easily fall through the cracks.

---

## Breakdown

**Who has the problem (specific user segment):**
Third-year Computer Science students at KIU who are simultaneously enrolled in multiple project-heavy courses.

**What exactly goes wrong (the pain):**
They experience continuous academic anxiety and suffer avoidable grade penalties resulting from missed instructions.

**When and where it happens (context):**
Throughout the semester, especially approaching midterms or finals, when professors distribute assignment updates, files, and deadlines across mismatched platforms (Moodle, MS Teams, email, and WhatsApp).

**What it costs them (consequence):**
Lost points/grades, compromised quality of academic work, and hours of wasted time spent manually collating requirements instead of studying.

**What they do about it today (current workaround):**
They manually perform daily "sweeps" of all university platforms and repeatedly ask, "Did anything new get posted?" in class WhatsApp/Messenger group chats.

**Why the workaround is inadequate:**
Group chats get noisy quickly, causing important messages to be buried, and manual platform sweeps are highly susceptible to human error and fatigue.
