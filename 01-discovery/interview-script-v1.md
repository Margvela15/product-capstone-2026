# Interview Script v1

**Team Name:** KIU Capstone Team  
**Problem this script is for:** Third-year CS students at KIU miss critical academic requirements and submissions due to overlapping and disjointed communication across Moodle, Teams, and WhatsApp.  
**ICP this script targets:** Primary ICP — 3rd-year CS student taking 3+ project-based courses, juggling multiple platforms.  
**Version:** v1.0  
**Date created:** 2026-03-24  
**Created by:** Archil Margvelashvili, Nikoloz Jvebenava, Giorgi Papidze, Giorgi Kveladze  

---

## Script Purpose
This script is a guide, not a rigid questionnaire. Use it to structure the conversation, but follow interesting threads when the interviewee takes you somewhere unexpected. The goal is to learn, not to complete a checklist.

**Every question in this script must pass the Mom Test:**
- Ask about past behaviour, not future intentions
- Ask about the problem, not your solution
- No leading questions
- No hypotheticals

---

## Screener Questions (Before the Interview)
Before scheduling an interview, confirm the person matches your ICP by asking these 2-3 screening questions over message or email. Do not interview people who do not match.

1. **[Screener question 1 — confirms segment match]**  
   *Are you currently a third-year Computer Science student at KIU taking multiple project-based courses?*

2. **[Screener question 2 — confirms context match]**  
   *Do you have active team projects or assignments actively running across multiple different courses right now?*

3. **[Screener question 3 — confirms the problem exists for them]**  
   *Have you ever found yourself frantically checking Moodle, Teams, or WhatsApp to figure out what you are supposed to submit or if you missed an update?*

---

## Interview Introduction (Deliver this, do not read it word-for-word)
"Thanks for taking the time to speak with me. I'm working on a university project where we're trying to understand how students manage their coursework and academic deadlines. I'm not pitching anything — I just want to understand how things work for you. There are no right or wrong answers. I might take some notes, but everything you tell me is just to help us understand the problem space better. Does that sound okay?

Is it okay if I record this or take notes?"

---

## Phase 1: Opening and Context (5 minutes — 2 questions minimum)
*Goal: Understand this person's world and daily routine in the relevant area. Do not mention your problem yet.*

**Question 1:**  
*Walk me through your routine when you sit down to tackle your university assignments for the week.*

**Question 2:**  
*How do you keep track of what needs to be done across your different courses?*

**Follow-up prompts to have ready:**
- "Can you tell me more about that?"
- "How long have you been doing it that way?"
- "Does that work well for you?"

---

## Phase 2: Problem Exploration (10 minutes — 3 questions minimum)
*Goal: Find out whether the problem shows up in their life without prompting it. Let them bring it up if possible.*

**Question 3:**  
*What are the most frustrating or stressful parts of keeping up with your academic workload right now?*

**Question 4:**  
*Is there any part of organizing your coursework where you feel like you are doing something tedious just to stay afloat?*

**Question 5:**  
*Tell me about a time recently when you almost missed—or actually missed—a deadline or an assignment requirement.*

*(If they mention your target problem unprompted: Note this — it is your strongest signal. Ask them to tell you more. Do not reveal that this is your focus.*  
*If they do not mention your target problem: It is okay to gently introduce the domain after Phase 2. "One thing I've been thinking about is how announcements and files are shared across different platforms. Has that ever been an issue for you?")*

---

## Phase 3: Problem Deep Dive (10 minutes — 3 questions minimum)
*Goal: Understand the problem with enough specificity and depth to write a meaningful problem statement and design accurate interview questions for future interviews.*

**Question 6:**  
*Tell me about the last time you missed an announcement or a formatting rule because it was posted somewhere unexpected. Walk me through it from beginning to end.*

**Question 7:**  
*When that happens, how do you usually verify what you need to do? (Follow-up: Why that approach? Why rely on that platform/person?)*

**Question 8:**  
*What happened as a result of that confusion? What did it cost you in terms of time, grades, or stress?*

*(Five Whys reminder: When they explain a behaviour, ask "why?" not to be annoying but to reach the real cause. Surface behaviours are symptoms. You want root causes.)*

---

## Phase 4: Current Solutions (5 minutes — 2 questions minimum)
*Goal: Understand exactly what they are currently using or doing to address this problem, and how satisfied they are.*

**Question 9:**  
*Have you ever tried anything—an app, a planner, a specific strategy—to consolidate all your course requirements? What happened?*

**Question 10:**  
*How well does checking Messenger groups and Moodle actually work for you? Where does that system break down?*

**Optional additional question (if applicable):**  
*(Normally: Have you ever paid for anything to help with this? Since this is a university problem, you might ask: "If there was a tool that perfectly organized this, how much of a difference would it actually make in your grade?")*

---

## Phase 5: Close (5 minutes — 1 question minimum)
*Goal: Gather any remaining insights, ask for referrals, and leave on good terms.*

**Question 11 (catch-all):**  
*Is there anything about how KIU courses are managed and communicated that I haven't asked you about, but that you think is important or frustrating?*

**Referral question:**  
*Is there anyone else you know who really struggles with this kind of thing, or someone who is super organized about it? I'd love to talk to them — would you be okay to introduce me?*

**Close:**  
*"This has been incredibly helpful. I really appreciate your time. If anything comes up that's relevant, would it be okay if I sent you a quick message?"*
